Recent advances in large language models (LLMs) have demonstrated remarkable capabilities in natural language understanding and generation. However, their application to specialized scientific domains remains challenging due to the need for domain-specific knowledge, accurate citation of sources, and the ability to reason over complex technical content. In this work, we propose SciAgent, a framework that combines retrieval-augmented generation with multi-step reasoning to assist researchers in literature review, hypothesis generation, and experimental design. Our approach integrates a dense retrieval module trained on scientific corpora with a planning agent that decomposes complex research questions into actionable sub-tasks. We evaluate SciAgent on three benchmarks spanning biomedicine, computer science, and materials science, demonstrating significant improvements over baseline LLM approaches in factual accuracy (23% improvement), citation precision (41% improvement), and research question quality as rated by domain experts. Our analysis reveals that the multi-step planning component is critical for handling interdisciplinary queries that require synthesizing information across multiple papers and domains.
